Summary

Merton Densher, who works nights at a newspaper, often spends time during the day in Kensington Gardens. He walks around without a clear destination, stopping, sitting, and walking again.

On several of these days, Kate Croy comes out of her aunt's house, crosses the road, and meets him in the Gardens. They then walk together or sit on chairs away from others.

They first met at a party in a gallery. After being introduced, they talked and looked at each other intently. Six months later, they saw each other on the Underground Railway. They exchanged looks, and Densher stayed on the train past his stop. He moved to sit next to her when a seat became available, and when she got off, he followed her. They walked together.

After this, Densher asked to visit her, and she agreed. He began visiting her at her aunt's, Mrs. Lowder's, house. Mrs. Lowder invited him to dinner and continued to receive his visits.

During one of their meetings in the park, Kate tells Densher she is sure her aunt will write to him and that he should be prepared. Densher asks if the aunt thinks he is not good enough for Kate, and Kate clarifies that the aunt thinks he is not good enough for her.

Kate then tells Densher about her family’s misfortunes, including her father's dishonor, the deaths of her two brothers, and her sister's unhappy marriage. She recounts how she learned of her father's disgrace as a girl without ever knowing the specific details. She tells Densher that she recently offered to go and live with her father to escape her aunt, but he refused her. Both her father and sister insist she must stay with her aunt to be of value to them.

Densher asks Kate to marry him the next day. She tells him to wait until after he has seen her aunt. He questions her, and she says he is also afraid of her aunt. She states that she will "try for everything" and not sacrifice him. She suggests they should wait and see what they can get from her aunt. Densher asks if his role is to win over the aunt. Kate impatiently tells him to "See her." He asks if he should grovel to her. In response, Kate tells him to do what he likes and walks away.
