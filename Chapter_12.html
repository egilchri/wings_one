Chapter 12

Chapter Summary & Character Study
Summary

Kate accompanies Milly to a doctor's appointment. The doctor, Sir Luke Strett, has only ten minutes but schedules another appointment with Milly in a day or two. After the brief meeting, Milly tells Kate that the doctor is a "dear," that he will take care of her, and that she is to see him again. Milly decides to attend the next appointment by herself and asks Kate for her discretion. While waiting for their carriage, Milly reassures Kate that she is satisfied and feels "absolved."

Two days later, Milly goes to her second appointment alone. The doctor asks if she was accompanied. She explains that she is alone, with no living family members. After examining her, the doctor gives her a small prescription and says he will visit her at her home in a few days. He instructs her to leave London after his visit and says she can go to the continent. He says he will "follow you up." Before she leaves, Milly asks if she will suffer, and he answers, "Not a bit." When she asks if she will live, he replies that this is what he is trying to persuade her to do.

Character Study

Based on the text, here is a concise character study:

  • *Milly Theale**
  • **Presentation:** Milly is presented as a highly perceptive, romantic, and emotionally volatile young woman who is isolated and grappling with a serious, unnamed affliction. She interprets her experiences through a rich inner monologue, transforming a brief medical appointment into a profound, almost spiritual event. She feels she is "loosely rattling" through life and craves a sense of firmness and order, even if it comes from a grim diagnosis.
  • **Situational Shift:** Milly enters the doctor's office anxious and overwhelmed. In ten minutes, her situation shifts dramatically: she feels an immediate, deep sense of trust and relief, as if she has been "absolved." She leaves the first meeting feeling she has gained an "absolute possession" in the form of the doctor's friendship. By the second meeting, this feeling solidifies into a paradoxical comfort; she accepts that his kindness implies her situation is "bad," yet finds a strange stability in being "found out" by his genius.
  • *Sir Luke Strett (The Doctor)**
  • **Presentation:** Sir Luke is portrayed through Milly’s eyes as a "great man" who is authoritative yet gentle. He projects an aura of profound, intuitive knowledge, seeming to understand Milly’s condition and character with minimal questioning ("He can tell. He knows"). He is described as looking like a "general" and a "bishop," combining firmness with a capacity for kindness that Milly finds comforting.
  • **Situational Shift:** Initially presented as a busy professional with only ten minutes to spare, his role quickly shifts to that of a dedicated, almost paternalistic guide. He commits to seeing Milly again, visiting her, and even following her abroad, moving beyond a standard doctor-patient relationship into one of "unlimited interest."
  • *Kate Croy**
  • **Presentation:** Kate is presented as a pragmatic and skeptical friend. Her concern is direct and physical, comparing Milly’s consultation to a tooth extraction ("Is it out?"). She presses for concrete facts ("what does he say?") and is suspicious of the doctor's rapid, intuitive assessment. Her friendship has a transactional quality; she demands "the truth" in exchange for her discretion.
  • **Situational Shift:** Kate begins as Milly's close companion, accompanying her to the appointment. Her situation shifts when Milly decides to see the doctor alone the next time, effectively moving Kate from an active participant to an outsider who is now only granted information at Milly's discretion.
