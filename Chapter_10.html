Chapter 10

Chapter Summary & Character Study
Summary

Lord Mark visited Milly and Susan Shepherd at their hotel on several occasions. He then brought them to a social gathering at a large historic house.

At the gathering, Milly was introduced to new people and walked through the house's halls. She accepted and drank a cup of iced coffee. During this time, Mrs. Lowder (Aunt Maud) told Milly she must stay with them and could stay in any position she wished.

Milly and Mrs. Lowder observed Kate Croy. Milly told Mrs. Lowder that Kate was the handsomest person there, lovely, and always right. Mrs. Lowder agreed that Kate was a luxury to have. As they talked, Lord Mark passed and repassed before them. A servant took Milly's empty ice-plate.

Mrs. Lowder continued speaking to Milly, urging her to make her home with them, to let them take care of her, and to help with Kate. She stated that Milly and Kate must stand together because together they could do anything, and that her plans for Kate also required Milly's prosperity.

Character Study

Based on the text, here is a concise character study:

  • *Lord Mark**
  • **Presentation:** He is presented as a persistent and subtly manipulative social operator. He seeks validation from Milly, wanting her to acknowledge his importance. He orchestrates the grand social experience for the women, acting as a "supervisory friend" while making it clear, non-verbally, that Milly is the true reason for his efforts. He is a key component in Mrs. Lowder's social maneuvering, a valuable piece she is "working in" to her design.
  • **Situational Shift:** His intention to impress Milly and correct her "injustice" has succeeded. He has successfully engineered a situation that has charmed her and created a "friendly understanding" between them, shifting him from a mere pursuer to an effective social power player in her eyes.
  • *Milly**
  • **Presentation:** She is highly observant, introspective, and impressionable, though with a sharp underlying awareness. She is physically described as a "poor shaky maiden" but possesses a rich internal life, viewing the social scene with a mix of awe and analytical curiosity. She feels overwhelmed by the grandeur but is perceptive enough to see the machinations of Lord Mark and Mrs. Lowder.
  • **Situational Shift:** Her situation shifts dramatically from being a guest at a party to becoming its focal point. Mrs. Lowder explicitly tells her she "is the matter," and that she can have any position she wants. This moves her from a passive observer to the designated centerpiece of the social plan, a realization that serves as a "climax" for her.
  • *Mrs. Lowder (Aunt Maud)**
  • **Presentation:** She is a powerful, effusive, and strategic woman, described as a "natural force." She is in a state of high satisfaction, seeing her plans and affections align. She uses grand, persuasive language to envelop Milly in her designs, seeing the girl's prosperity as essential to her niece Kate's. She is actively weaving the people around her into a "free and genial" but definite plan.
  • **Situational Shift:** Her plans "enlarge and uplift." Having successfully brought Milly into this grand setting, she shifts from mere hospitality to an open campaign to secure Milly's permanent place within her circle, making her happiness and future integral to the family's social success.
  • *Kate Croy**
  • **Presentation:** She is presented through Milly’s admiring perspective as a beautiful, enigmatic, and socially perfect figure. She has the ability to appear as a "beautiful stranger," making her a constant subject of curiosity. Milly sees her as being "made for great social uses" and always "so right." For Mrs. Lowder, she is a prized asset, a "luxury to take about the world."
  • **Situational Shift:** Her situation is not shifted directly but is powerfully reinforced. The successful integration of Milly into their circle solidifies Kate’s own position, as Mrs. Lowder’s plan now requires both young women to prosper together.
  • *Susan Shepherd (Susie)**
  • **Presentation:** She is a gentle, secondary figure who is valued for her role as a connector. Lord Mark is merely "civil" to her, and Mrs. Lowder views her sentimentally as a miraculously restored old friend, kept "in lavender and pink paper." Her main function in the scene is as the catalyst for the friendship between Milly and Kate.
  • **Situational Shift:** There is no shift in her situation; she remains a static, pleasant presence who appreciates the experience on behalf of the group.
