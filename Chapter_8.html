Chapter 8

Chapter Summary & Character Study
Summary

For a week following a dinner, an American pair, Milly Theale and her companion Mrs. Stringham (Susan Shepherd), are in London. Milly and Mrs. Stringham frequently remark to each other that they have only themselves to thank. Mrs. Stringham is pleased because an appeal she made to her old schoolmate, Mrs. Lowder (Maud Manningham), was met positively.

During this week, the two elder women, Mrs. Stringham and Mrs. Lowder, spend time together. Mrs. Stringham tells Mrs. Lowder about Milly, and Mrs. Lowder tells Mrs. Stringham about her niece, Kate Croy.

At the same time, Milly and Kate Croy become friends and spend their days together. They explore London's shops, streets, suburbs, museums, and monuments. Milly gives Mrs. Stringham accounts of her time with Kate. She tells Mrs. Stringham she thinks Kate has a secret trouble and is using their friendship as a diversion.

Kate tells Milly about her family situation. In turn, Milly tells Kate about her American background, her family's financial history, and her deceased relatives. Each tells the other she finds her more remarkable than herself.

During their outings, Milly gives Kate several presents, including precious ornaments. Kate tells Milly she must avoid shops unless Milly promises not to buy things for her.

By the end of the week, Milly asks Kate for information about Lord Mark and to be promised a visit to Kate's sister, Mrs. Condrip. Kate explains that Lord Mark's value is based on his future potential, which her aunt believes in. She says he was briefly in Parliament but lost his seat. Kate also explains to Milly that in London society, relationships are like bargains where everyone gives something to get something in return. She says that both she and Milly have been "taken up" by Mrs. Lowder.

In another conversation, Milly observes to Kate that she is surprised Mrs. Lowder is not bored by Mrs. Stringham. Kate agrees, and Milly sees that Kate finds Mrs. Stringham uninteresting.

Character Study

Based on the text, here is a concise character study:

  • *Milly Theale**
  • **Presentation:** An immensely wealthy, curious, and imaginative American, Milly is initially presented as a "slightly gasping" newcomer, likened to a "wandering princess." She perceives the world through a romantic and literary lens, immediately casting Kate as a "heroine." She is perceptive, guessing Kate has a secret trouble, yet naive about social dangers. She is extremely generous and possesses a freedom from financial anxiety that Kate finds remarkable.
  • **Situational Shift:** She moves from being a passive observer "caught up by a wave" to an active and engaged participant in London life. Her initial awe shifts to a deep fascination, and she quickly forms an intense friendship with Kate, even beginning to see her own powerful status through Kate’s eyes.
  • *Mrs. Stringham (Susan "Susie" Shepherd)**
  • **Presentation:** Milly’s older companion, a "pilgrim from Boston." She is analytical and values understanding the reasons behind actions. She is initially in a state of "exaltation" that her social faith in Mrs. Lowder has been justified. This gives way to a feeling of being "carried away" by the "vast, obscure, lurid" London world, which gives her a "bad conscience."
  • **Situational Shift:** Her situation shifts from one of simple, triumphant success to a more complex state of being both fascinated and morally unnerved by her new environment. She is simultaneously thrilled for Milly and overwhelmed herself.
  • *Kate Croy**
  • **Presentation:** A "handsome English girl," she is presented as pragmatic, worldly, and observant. Milly sees her as the archetype of the "wondrous London girl," but Kate sees herself as part of a transactional social system where people are either "working" or "being worked." She has an unspoken "smothered trouble." She is described as having a defensive "brutality" and a quickness to judge what she will not like.
  • **Situational Shift:** Initially positioned as a guide for Milly, Kate quickly becomes an intimate friend and a powerful influence. She shifts from being an object of Milly’s romantic imagination to an active force who provides a cynical but frank education in the ways of her world.
  • *Mrs. Lowder (Maud Manningham)**
  • **Presentation:** A formidable and powerful London hostess. She is described as "large" not because she is empty but because she is "full," like a "projectile... loaded and ready for use." She is a woman of action who operates on instinct rather than articulated reasons. She is a social force who "takes people up" and in whom others, like Kate, place their belief.
  • **Situational Shift:** Her primary shift occurred just before this text begins by unexpectedly and graciously welcoming her old American friends. Within the passage, she is a stable center of power, actively managing her social circle and setting events in motion for the other characters.
