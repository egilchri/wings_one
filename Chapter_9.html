Chapter 9

Chapter Summary & Character Study
Summary

During a drive in Battersea Park, Susan (Susie) Shepherd learns from Mrs. Lowder that Mrs. Lowder and her niece, Kate Croy, are acquainted with Milly's friend, Mr. Merton Densher. Later, Susie tells Milly this news. Milly acts surprised. Susie explains that Mrs. Lowder mentioned a young journalist who had gone to America, and Susie confirmed it was Mr. Densher, an acquaintance of Milly's.

Milly tells Susie that she did the right thing in revealing the connection. Susie then relays a message from Mrs. Lowder: Milly should not mention Mr. Densher to Kate unless Kate brings him up first. Milly asks if there is something between Kate and Mr. Densher. Susie says she thinks not, but Mrs. Lowder is being cautious. Susie adds that Mrs. Lowder wants her niece to marry Lord Mark.

For the next few days, Milly observes Kate, knowing that Kate also knows Mr. Densher, but says nothing to her about it.

Milly and Kate visit Kate’s sister, Mrs. Condrip. Later that evening, Milly tells Susie what happened during the visit. While Kate was out of the room, Mrs. Condrip told Milly that Mr. Densher is in love with Kate, which she considers a dreadful situation because of his poverty. Mrs. Condrip asked Milly to help do something about it and also asked Milly not to tell Kate about their conversation. Milly did not tell Mrs. Condrip that she herself knew Mr. Densher.

Milly tells Susie that according to Mrs. Condrip, Mrs. Lowder disapproves of Mr. Densher. Susie corrects this, saying Mrs. Lowder actually likes him and thinks he is very able. Milly and Susie discuss the situation. Milly believes that if Mrs. Condrip knew that Milly also knew Densher, she would tell Kate in order to make Kate jealous and turn her against him. Milly concludes the conversation by clarifying that Mrs. Condrip only said that Densher was in love with Kate, not that Kate was in love with him. She says that if Kate were in love, Mrs. Condrip would have said so.

Character Study

Based on the text, here is a concise character study:

  • *Milly Theale**
  • **Presentation:** Milly is presented as a character with a stark public/private divide. In public, she can be "detached as the occasion required," but in private, she is deeply intimate and open with her confidante, Susan Shepherd. She is wealthy, observant, and possesses a "strange gaiety" coupled with a fascination for social complexity, stating, "I want abysses." She also displays a morbid, fatalistic humor regarding a fragile health condition.
  • **Situational Shift:** Her situation shifts dramatically with the news that her new friend, Kate Croy, and her circle already know her recent acquaintance, Merton Densher. This revelation "altered all proportions" for her, turning an amusing coincidence into a "new sort of fun" containing "a small element of anxiety." She immediately becomes a participant in a social intrigue, agreeing to keep her knowledge of Densher a secret from Kate.
  • *Susan Shepherd (Susie/Mrs. Stringham)**
  • **Presentation:** She is Milly's loyal, devoted, and sometimes "venerably simple" companion. She serves as Milly's primary confidante and feels a protective, almost maternal duty towards her ("I'd die for you"). She is presented as a somewhat flustered navigator of the complex English social world she has been "rapt" into.
  • **Situational Shift:** Her situation shifts from passive companion to active agent in the plot. By relaying the news about Densher from Mrs. Lowder, and later receiving an unsolicited confession from Mrs. Condrip, she becomes the keeper of multiple secrets and a key conduit of information between the other characters.
  • *Kate Croy**
  • **Presentation:** Kate is presented almost entirely through Milly's changing perception. Initially, she is a "handsome" new friend with whom Milly shares a "deep" and "sudden" intimacy. She is also positioned as being under the social control of her aunt, "involved in her aunt's designs."
  • **Situational Shift:** With the revelation of her connection to Densher, she is immediately recast in Milly's mind as a person with a hidden, "not wholly calculable" side. Her silence on the subject of Densher transforms her from a transparent friend into an enigma, surrounded by a "great darkness."
  • *Merton Densher**
  • **Presentation:** He is an off-stage character whose identity is built through the conflicting reports of others. He is a "young journalist" who is described by Mrs. Condrip as poor and lacking prospects, but by Mrs. Lowder as "exceptionally able."
  • **Situational Shift:** He is abruptly transformed from a minor New York acquaintance of Milly's into the central figure of a complex social secret linking all the main characters. His prior silence about his English friends is suddenly rendered significant.
  • *Mrs. Lowder (Aunt Maud)**
  • **Presentation:** She is a formidable and manipulative social strategist. She is wealthy, powerful, and intent on controlling her niece's future, specifically by arranging a marriage to Lord Mark. She is presented as shrewdly precautionary, immediately moving to control the flow of information about Densher.
  • **Situational Shift:** The unexpected appearance of Milly, who also knows Densher, introduces an unknown variable into her carefully managed plans for Kate, prompting her immediate directive for silence.
  • *Mrs. Condrip (Marian)**
  • **Presentation:** Kate's sister, she lives in a lower social sphere ("in quite another geography"). She is portrayed as worried, perhaps meddlesome, and unimpressed by her sister's world.
  • **Situational Shift:** She acts as an unplanned catalyst. By impulsively revealing to Milly that Densher is in love with Kate and that she considers the match "dreadful," she confirms the romantic nature of the secret and escalates the intrigue.
